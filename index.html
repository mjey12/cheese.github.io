African Wild Dog Packs
Africa wild dogs' status on the ICUN Redlist is currently Endagered. Their population is rapidly declining, as alpha females and alpha males are usually the only ones to reproduce. African wild dogs usually travel in packs of ten, however some packs can hold up to 40 wild dogs. They hunt gazelles, antelopes, warthogs, and more.
Did you know that African wild dogs have 10 different common names? These names are cape hunting dog, african hunting dog, hunting dog, african wild dog, wild dog, hyena dog, painted dog, painted hunting dog, painted wolf, and ornate wolf. African hunting dogs are also known as painted wolves because of their many colors of fur.
African wild dogs often live usually live in arid zones and in the savanna. They can also be found in woodlands and mountains if there is plentiful prey there.
African wild dogs keep certain animal species from overpopulationg, such as warthogs and gazelles. They are also a natural prey to carnivorous animals such as lion and hyena.

The African wild dog population has been rapidly decreasing at a rate of 1,000 wild dogs per year. They have disappeared from 25 of the 39 countries in which they were previously found in.
